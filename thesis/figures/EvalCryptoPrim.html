Evaluation of Cryptographic Primitives
Level of Security Difficult to quantify, but usually given by an upper bound in terms of the number of operations necessary to defeat a given security objective.
Functionality To meet a particular security objective often cryptographic primitives will need to be combined. The primitives chosen should be those with the most effective properties with respect to the objective.
Method of Operation Behavior of a primitive is dependent upon the application and the input. Since a primitiveâ€™s functional capabilities are determined by this behavior, to satisfy a particular objective the mode of operation should be considered carefully when choosing a primitive.
Performance The efficiency of a given primitive under a certain mode of operation.
Ease of Implementation Referes to the difficulty in applying the primitive in a specific application. Sometimes given in terms of the complexity of the primitive over a software or hardware application.
